We can't stop climate change without ending deforestation. Forests store millions of tons of carbon, but we're losing 40 football fields a day. Stand For Trees helps to change the economic incentives that lead to deforestation, and our member projects each protect a threatened forest in an incredibly biodiverse area of the world.

Forests do many, many things. They clean air and water, help to prevent soil erosion and to protect against natural disasters, provide building materials, foods, medicines, and much more for humans, and create habitat for wildlife. Stand For Trees helps to change the economic incentives that lead to a vicious cycle of deforestation and degradation.

Forests are some of the most diverse ecosystems on earth. And everything in them depends on the rest of the ecosystem, from worms and insects that make the soil richer, to trees that provide shade, food, and territory. The more biodiversity, the stronger it is. That's why protecting intact forests - like those in Stand For Trees projects - is so important.

Forests in the developing world are critical to the lives of the communities that call them home. All Stand For Trees projects have earned special certifications for supporting community development, poverty reduction, and sustainable livelihoods - giving communities a sustainable alternative to clearing their forests.

Tropical forests are home to thousands of species of animals, birds, and fish, including many endangered species. Our projects have all earned special certifications for protecting wildlife.
